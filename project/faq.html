Frequently Asked Questions

We have compiled answers to the most common questions about Medical Safe Charity Foundation. If you cannot find what you are looking for, please reach out to our team.

Medical Safe Charity Foundation is dedicated to advancing healthcare access and improving health outcomes for underserved communities. We accomplish this through medical missions, health education programs, emergency response initiatives, maternal and child health services, and clean water projects. Our vision is a world where every individual, regardless of geography or socioeconomic status, has access to quality healthcare and the opportunity to live a healthy, dignified life.

Donations to Medical Safe Charity Foundation are used to fund medical missions, purchase essential medicines and equipment, support health education workshops, provide emergency relief supplies, and sustain our administrative operations. We are committed to transparency and ensure that the majority of every dollar donated goes directly to program delivery. A detailed breakdown of our spending is available in our annual report, which we publish on our website and share with donors upon request.

Absolutely! While we greatly value medical professionals, we welcome volunteers from all backgrounds. Non-medical volunteers play a vital role in patient registration, community outreach, translation services, logistics support, fundraising, administrative tasks, and health education. We provide comprehensive training for all roles, and no prior medical experience is required. Your willingness to serve and your unique skills are what matter most to us.

Applying to volunteer is simple. You can fill out the volunteer registration form on our Volunteer page, which asks for your contact details, area of interest, availability, and a brief message. Once submitted, our volunteer coordination team will review your application and reach out within 48 hours to discuss available opportunities, answer your questions, and guide you through the onboarding process. We also conduct orientation sessions for all new volunteers before they join any mission.

Yes, Medical Safe Charity Foundation is a registered nonprofit organization, and all donations are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law. After making a donation, you will automatically receive a receipt via email that serves as your official record for tax purposes. If you need a physical copy or have any questions about tax documentation, please contact our donor relations team, and we will be happy to assist you.

Medical Safe Charity Foundation currently operates in several regions across West Africa, including rural and peri-urban communities in Ghana, Nigeria, Sierra Leone, and Liberia. We also have ongoing programs in select communities in East Africa. Our operations are concentrated in areas with limited healthcare infrastructure, high poverty rates, and significant unmet medical needs. We continuously assess new regions for potential expansion based on community needs and available resources.

Yes, we welcome donors who wish to sponsor a specific program or initiative. Whether you want to fund a medical mission, support a health education workshop, provide clean water infrastructure for a community, or underwrite maternal health services, we can tailor a sponsorship package to your interests. Please contact our development team to discuss your preferences, and we will work with you to create a meaningful sponsorship arrangement with regular updates on impact.

There are several ways to stay connected with Medical Safe Charity Foundation. You can subscribe to our newsletter through the form on our website to receive monthly updates on programs, impact stories, and volunteer opportunities. We are also active on social media platforms including Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, and YouTube, where we share real-time updates from the field. Additionally, we publish an annual impact report and periodic blog posts on our website.

Medical Safe Charity Foundation provides community-based healthcare for patients with diabetes, hypertension and chronic conditions — regardless of background.
