Featured Projects

From mobile clinics to clean water initiatives, our high-impact projects are creating lasting change in underserved communities across the globe.

2025 Initiative

Mobile Clinic Fleet Expansion

We are deploying 5 new fully equipped mobile medical units to reach remote villages across East Africa. Each vehicle serves as a complete primary care clinic on wheels, stocked with examination equipment, laboratory diagnostics, telemedicine capabilities, and a pharmacy. The fleet will reach 40 previously underserved communities, serving approximately 50,000 patients annually. Local healthcare workers will staff each unit alongside volunteer physicians, ensuring culturally competent care and knowledge transfer to community health systems.

Ongoing Program

Community Health Worker Training

Our flagship training program has equipped over 200 community health workers with essential skills in preventive care, nutrition counseling, maternal and child health, disease surveillance, and basic emergency response. These workers are selected from the communities they serve, ensuring deep trust and cultural alignment. Each graduate is equipped with a medical kit, a smartphone loaded with health tracking software, and ongoing mentorship from Medical Safe medical supervisors. The program has contributed to a 40% reduction in preventable disease incidence across target districts.

2024 Completion

Clean Water for 30 Communities

In partnership with local governments and international donors, we successfully installed 50 water points across 30 rural communities, providing clean, safe drinking water to over 75,000 people. The project included deep borehole drilling with solar-powered pumps, rainwater harvesting systems on school and clinic roofs, household-level ceramic water filters, and community-led total sanitation training. Waterborne disease rates have dropped by 65% in the region, school attendance has risen sharply, and women and girls report significant time savings that are now spent on education and income-generating activities.

Medical Mission

Bringing Sight Back to 500 Patients

In March 2024, our team of ophthalmologists and volunteer surgeons set up a week-long cataract surgery camp in the rural district of Busia, Kenya. For many of the 500 patients who received free sight-restoring surgery, it was the first time they had seen the faces of their loved ones clearly in years. Among them was 67-year-old Grace Akinyi, who had been blind for nearly a decade. "I had given up hope of ever seeing my grandchildren grow up," she said. "Now I can watch them play, and I can weave baskets again to support my family." The mission not only restored vision but also restored dignity, independence, and economic opportunity.

Community Impact

From Village to Health Worker

Fatima Diallo was a 24-year-old mother of three living in a remote village in Senegal when she was selected for our Community Health Worker Training Program. With no formal education beyond primary school, Fatima never imagined she would become a healthcare provider. After completing the six-month training, she now serves 15 surrounding villages, providing prenatal care, immunizations, and health education. She has delivered 40 healthy babies, treated over 200 cases of childhood malaria, and taught more than 500 women about family planning and nutrition. "I am proof that when you invest in a woman, you invest in an entire community," Fatima says.

Health Education

A School Without Water

St. Mary's Primary School in northern Uganda had 800 students but no clean water source. Children walked 3 miles each day to fetch water from a contaminated river, missing class and falling ill with waterborne diseases. Absentee rates hovered at 35%. Through our Clean Water for Schools initiative, we installed a solar-powered borehole, rain catchment system, and handwashing stations. Within six months, absenteeism dropped to under 5%, and the school reported zero cases of cholera or dysentery for the first time in its history. Enrollment has since grown to 950 students, and neighboring communities now draw from the same water point.
